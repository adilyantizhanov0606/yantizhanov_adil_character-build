James Hetfield - Metallica

James Hetfield (born August 3, 1963) is an American musician best known as the co-founder, lead vocalist, rhythm guitarist, and primary songwriter of the legendary heavy-metal band Metallica. Since forming the band with drummer Lars Ulrich in 1981, Hetfield has been central to shaping Metallica’s sound, helping transform the group from a fast underground thrash act into one of the best-selling and most influential rock bands in history.

Dave Mustaine - Megadeth

Dave Mustaine is an American musician best known as the founder, lead vocalist, guitarist, and primary songwriter of the thrash-metal band Megadeth. Born on September 13, 1961, he first gained recognition as an early member of Metallica, contributing to the band’s formative sound before being dismissed in 1983. His departure from Metallica motivated him to form Megadeth, which quickly became one of the “Big Four” thrash-metal bands alongside Metallica, Slayer, and Anthrax.

Kerry King - Slayer

Kerry King is an American guitarist and songwriter best known as one of the founding members of the legendary thrash-metal band Slayer. Born on June 3, 1964, he helped form Slayer in 1981 alongside Jeff Hanneman, and the band went on to become one of the “Big Four” of thrash metal, alongside Metallica, Megadeth, and Anthrax. King’s aggressive playing style and stage presence became defining elements of the band’s identity.

Ian Scott - Anthrax

Scott Ian is an American musician best known as the rhythm guitarist, co-founder, and primary lyricist of the thrash metal band Anthrax, one of the “Big Four” of thrash metal (alongside Metallica, Megadeth, and Slayer).
